WHO WE ARE

Doctors For All is an organisation combating medical access.

Our vision is to develop SELF-SUSTAINABLE HEALTH CARE by fostering long-term partnerships with local communities in areas devastated by poverty, war, and disease through education, improving access to healthcare, and meeting basic health care needs.

PROJECT SOMALILAND

WHAT IS SOMALILAND?

Somaliland is a region of Somalia found in the north of the country. It is a self-declared independent state which has arisen from 30 years of civil war. It has actively been seeking recognition since 1991.This region was left devastated by a 30 year civil war with other parts of Somalia. This prompted it to try gain recognition as an independent country in 1991. The region is reliant on livestock and crops for the majority of its income and consequently foreign aid plays a very important role.

WHAT WE'RE DOING

We will work in conjunction with Troppodoc and SHiFAT to provide basic healthcare to hard to reach areas in Somaliland. Through clinics aimed at managing the most common treatable diseases, we hope to provide education, promote health, and develop long-term partnerships. The aim will be to eventually leave with a running self-sustainable healthcare system in areas that had minimal access to healthcare.
